# ANXA2R

## Overview
The ANXA2R gene encodes the annexin A2 receptor, a protein that functions as a receptor involved in various cellular processes, including membrane trafficking, cell signaling, and cytoskeletal interactions. This receptor is primarily localized in the plasma membrane and cytoplasm, where it plays a crucial role in organizing cellular structures and mediating responses to extracellular signals. ANXA2R is integral to endocytosis and exocytosis, processes that are essential for the internalization and secretion of molecules, thereby maintaining cellular homeostasis and facilitating intercellular communication. The receptor's influence on cell surface dynamics is critical for cell proliferation, migration, tissue development, repair, and immune responses. Additionally, its role in cytoskeletal interactions is vital for maintaining cell shape and stability, ensuring proper cellular function and movement. Through these diverse functions, ANXA2R is essential for cellular organization and adaptability, contributing significantly to the health and functionality of human tissues (Qiu2020aarFDC).
