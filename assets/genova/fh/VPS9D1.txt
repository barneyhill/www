# VPS9D1

## Overview
VPS9D1 is a gene that encodes the protein VPS9 domain containing 1, which plays a crucial role in intracellular trafficking. This protein is characterized by the presence of a VPS9 domain, which is instrumental in the activation of Rab GTPases, key regulators of vesicular transport processes within the cell. The VPS9D1 protein is involved in various cellular processes, including the regulation of vesicle formation, movement, and fusion, which are essential for maintaining cellular homeostasis. The protein's structure, including its secondary and tertiary conformations, is vital for its interaction with other molecules and its functional role in cellular signaling pathways. Additionally, post-translational modifications such as phosphorylation may influence its activity and interactions, highlighting its regulatory potential in cellular dynamics (Qiu2020aarFDC).
