# TXNDC2

## Overview
TXNDC2 is a gene that encodes the protein thioredoxin domain containing 2, which is a member of the thioredoxin family known for its role in redox regulation. This protein functions as an oxidoreductase, primarily involved in the reduction of disulfide bonds, thereby maintaining cellular redox homeostasis. TXNDC2 is predominantly expressed in the testis and plays a critical role in spermatogenesis, particularly during the haploid phase, by participating in chromatin protamination and sperm chromatin condensation. The protein's activity is essential for proper protein folding and stabilization, contributing to cellular health and function. Additionally, TXNDC2 has been implicated in male infertility and certain cancer pathologies, highlighting its clinical significance (Dong2021Mutational; Javadirad2021TXNDC2; Javadirad2021Sperm).

## Function
TXNDC2, or thioredoxin domain containing 2, is a gene that encodes a protein involved in redox regulation within cells. This protein functions as an oxidoreductase, facilitating the reduction of disulfide bonds in other proteins, which is crucial for maintaining cellular redox homeostasis. TXNDC2 is primarily active in the testis, where it plays a significant role during spermatogenesis, particularly in the haploid phase. It is expressed in round and elongating spermatids and is involved in chromatin protamination, a process essential for sperm chromatin condensation and male fertility (Javadirad2021Sperm).

The protein is involved in the regulation of redox signaling, which is crucial for sperm fertility, and acts as a sperm-specific oxidoreductase (Javadirad2021TXNDC2). TXNDC2 is a nuclear protein that participates in the oxidation of protamine monomers, which is necessary for protamine activation and DNA binding during spermatogenesis (Javadirad2021TXNDC2). The synchronous downregulation of TXNDC2 and protamine genes PRM1 and PRM2 in severe sperm failure phenotypes highlights its importance in healthy sperm production (Javadirad2021Sperm). The protein's activity is crucial for the proper folding and stabilization of proteins, contributing to cellular health and function.

## Clinical Significance
The TXNDC2 gene has been implicated in male infertility, particularly in cases of azoospermia, a condition characterized by the absence of sperm in semen. Studies have shown that TXNDC2, along with protamines PRM1 and PRM2, is significantly downregulated in various pathological phenotypes of azoospermia, such as Sertoli cell-only syndrome (SCOS), pre-meiotic arrest, and meiotic arrest. This downregulation is associated with impaired spermatogenesis and male infertility, suggesting that TXNDC2 could serve as a molecular marker for predicting sperm retrieval success in azoospermic men (Javadirad2021Sperm; Javadirad2021TXNDC2).

In the context of cancer, a missense mutation (G207S) in the TXNDC2 protein has been identified in invasive adenocarcinoma samples, which may contribute to the development and progression of lung cancer by affecting cellular functions and pathways related to transcription regulation and protein phosphorylation (Dong2021Mutational). These findings highlight the potential clinical significance of TXNDC2 in both reproductive health and cancer, indicating its role in disease pathogenesis through altered expression or mutations.
