# ATP5F1D

## Overview
The ATP5F1D gene encodes the delta subunit of the mitochondrial ATP synthase, a key enzyme complex involved in cellular energy production through oxidative phosphorylation. ATP synthase, also known as Complex V, is essential for synthesizing adenosine triphosphate (ATP) from adenosine diphosphate (ADP) and inorganic phosphate, a process critical for maintaining cellular energy homeostasis. The delta subunit, produced by ATP5F1D, is a component of the central stalk of the ATP synthase complex, playing a crucial role in the structural integrity and function of the enzyme. This subunit is integral to the assembly and stability of the ATP synthase complex, facilitating the rotational mechanism necessary for ATP synthesis. Mutations in ATP5F1D can lead to mitochondrial disorders characterized by impaired energy metabolism, underscoring the gene's importance in cellular and organismal energy balance (Oláhová2018Biallelic; Tauchmannová2024Variability).

## Function
The ATP5F1D gene encodes the delta subunit of ATP synthase, a critical enzyme in the mitochondrial respiratory chain responsible for ATP production. ATP synthase, also known as Complex V, plays a vital role in cellular energy metabolism by synthesizing ATP from ADP and inorganic phosphate during oxidative phosphorylation. The delta subunit, encoded by ATP5F1D, is part of the central stalk of the ATP synthase complex, which is essential for the enzyme's structural integrity and function (Oláhová2018Biallelic; Tauchmannová2024Variability).

In healthy human cells, ATP5F1D is crucial for the proper assembly and stability of the ATP synthase complex. It ensures efficient ATP production necessary for various cellular processes, supporting energy metabolism and maintaining cellular energy homeostasis (Oláhová2018Biallelic; Tauchmannová2024Variability). The delta subunit, along with the gamma and epsilon subunits, forms the central stalk, which stabilizes the connection between the catalytic F1 part and the membrane-embedded Fo part of the enzyme, facilitating the rotational mechanism required for ATP synthesis (Tauchmannová2024Variability; Ganapathi2022A). This process is vital for energy metabolism, supporting various cellular functions and overall organismal energy homeostasis (Oláhová2018Biallelic).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations in the ATP5F1D gene, which encodes a subunit of ATP synthase, are associated with a range of mitochondrial disorders. These disorders often manifest as metabolic decompensation, characterized by symptoms such as lactic acidosis, hyperammonemia, and 3-methylglutaconic aciduria. Patients may also experience muscle weakness, developmental delays, and episodic metabolic crises (Oláhová2018Biallelic; Tauchmannová2024Variability).

Specific pathogenic variants in ATP5F1D, such as the homozygous c.245C>T (p.Pro82Leu) and c.317T>G (p.Val106Gly), have been identified in patients with these conditions. These variants lead to impaired assembly and function of the ATP synthase complex, resulting in reduced ATP production and mitochondrial dysfunction (Oláhová2018Biallelic; Tauchmannová2024Variability). The clinical presentation can vary significantly, with some individuals experiencing severe symptoms from birth, while others may remain asymptomatic until later in childhood (Zastrow2019A; Tauchmannová2024Variability).

Functional studies in model organisms, such as Drosophila, have demonstrated that these mutations cause a partial loss of function in ATP synthase, further supporting their pathogenicity (Oláhová2018Biallelic). These findings underscore the critical role of ATP5F1D in maintaining mitochondrial function and energy metabolism.

## Interactions
The ATP5F1D gene encodes a subunit of the mitochondrial ATP synthase complex, specifically the F1 d subunit, which is integral to the assembly and function of complex V of the mitochondrial respiratory chain. This subunit interacts with other components of the ATP synthase complex, such as ATP5F1A and ATP5F1B, to facilitate the conversion of ADP to ATP. Biallelic mutations in ATP5F1D, including the missense variants p.Pro82Leu and p.Val106Gly, disrupt these interactions, leading to impaired assembly of the F1FO ATP synthase complex and reduced complex V activity (Oláhová2018Biallelic).

These mutations result in an inability of ATP5F1D to bind correctly with other F1 subunits, which is crucial for the proper assembly and function of the ATP synthase complex. This was demonstrated in Drosophila models, where mutant proteins failed to rescue phenotypic defects, highlighting the importance of ATP5F1D's physical interactions with other subunits (Oláhová2018Biallelic). The study also observed that these mutations do not affect the steady-state amounts of ATP5F1D but lead to a decrease in other complex V subunits, further indicating a disruption in the physical interactions within the ATP synthase complex (Oláhová2018Biallelic).
