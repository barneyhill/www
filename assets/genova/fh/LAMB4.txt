# LAMB4

## Overview
LAMB4 is a gene that encodes the laminin subunit beta 4, a component of the laminin family of extracellular matrix glycoproteins. Laminins are heterotrimeric proteins composed of α, β, and γ chains, and they play essential roles in cell adhesion, differentiation, and tissue architecture (Miner2004LAMININ). The LAMB4 protein, as a β-subunit, contributes to the structural integrity and function of the extracellular matrix, particularly within the gastrointestinal tract. It is predominantly expressed in the colon, where it is localized to the myenteric plexus, suggesting a role in regulating gut motility and supporting the enteric nervous system (Coble2017Identification). Alterations in LAMB4 expression or function have been implicated in various diseases, including gastrointestinal disorders and cancers, highlighting its significance in maintaining normal cellular and tissue functions (Domogatskaya2012Functional; Choi2014Laminin).

## Function
The LAMB4 gene encodes a β-subunit of laminin, a family of heterotrimeric proteins crucial for the structural integrity of the extracellular matrix. In healthy human cells, LAMB4 is selectively expressed in the colon, where it plays a significant role in the enteric nervous system. Immunohistochemical analysis has shown that LAMB4 protein is strongly localized to the myenteric plexus within the colon, specifically to the endoneurium, which is consistent with its role in supporting the function of the enteric nervous system (Coble2017Identification).

Laminins, including those containing the LAMB4 subunit, are involved in cell adhesion, differentiation, migration, and resistance to apoptosis. These proteins interact with cell membrane receptors, influencing signaling pathways that are essential for cellular processes such as differentiation and morphogenesis (Domogatskaya2012Functional). Although the specific laminin complexes containing LAMB4 have not been fully characterized, its expression pattern suggests a role in maintaining the structural and functional integrity of the gastrointestinal tract (Coble2017Identification).

The absence or mutation of laminin subunits can lead to severe diseases, highlighting the importance of LAMB4 in maintaining normal cellular and tissue functions (Domogatskaya2012Functional).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and altered expression of the LAMB4 gene have been implicated in the pathogenesis of gastric cancer (GC) and colorectal cancer (CRC). LAMB4 is frequently somatically mutated in these cancers, particularly in cases with high microsatellite instability (MSI-H). These mutations are often frameshift mutations that lead to premature stop codons, resulting in a loss of function of the LAMB4 protein. Such mutations were identified in 11.8% of GC and 7.6% of CRC cases with MSI-H, but were absent in cases with microsatellite stability (MSS) or low microsatellite instability (MSI-L) (Choi2014Laminin).

The loss of LAMB4 expression, observed in 17-32% of GC and CRC cases, is associated with these mutations and MSI-H status, suggesting a potential role as a tumor suppressor gene. The altered expression of LAMB4 in these cancers may contribute to tumor progression by affecting cell adhesion and the structural integrity of the basement membrane (Choi2014Laminin).

In addition to its role in GC and CRC, LAMB4 is associated with poor prognosis in several cancers and is involved in cancer-related pathways such as epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT). Its expression is linked to immune cell infiltration and immunotherapy response, indicating its potential as a prognostic biomarker and therapeutic target (Tao2022Integrative).
