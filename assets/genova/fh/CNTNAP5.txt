# CNTNAP5

## Overview
CNTNAP5, or contactin associated protein family member 5, is a gene that encodes a neural transmembrane protein belonging to the neurexin superfamily. This protein is primarily involved in cell adhesion and communication within the nervous system, playing a crucial role in neurite formation, neuronal development, and axonal guidance (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS). The protein is characterized by domains typical of cell adhesion molecules, such as laminin G, EGF-like, and fibronectin type III, which facilitate interactions between cells and the extracellular matrix, essential for neural tissue integrity and function. CNTNAP5 is predominantly expressed in myelinated axons and is enriched in retinal and neural tissues, indicating its specialized role in these areas (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS). The gene's involvement in synaptic organization and function underscores its importance in maintaining neural connectivity and signaling, with implications for various neurological and retinal diseases (Lengyel2021Chromosome).

## Structure
CNTNAP5, or contactin associated protein family member 5, is a member of the neurexin superfamily, which is involved in cell adhesion and communication within the nervous system. The protein is characterized by the presence of several domains that are typical of cell adhesion molecules, including laminin G, EGF-like, and fibronectin type III domains. These domains contribute to the protein's role in mediating interactions between cells and the extracellular matrix, which is essential for maintaining the structural integrity and function of neural tissues.

## Function
CNTNAP5, or contactin-associated protein-like 5, is a neural transmembrane protein that is part of the neurexin superfamily, primarily active in the brain. It plays a critical role in neurite formation, neuronal development, axonal domain organization, and axonal guidance (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS). CNTNAP5 is mainly enriched in myelinated axons and functions as a cell adhesion molecule within the Contactin family. Its absence can lead to axon malformation and poor nerve transmission, highlighting its importance in maintaining proper neural connectivity and signaling (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS).

The gene is involved in synaptic organization and function, contributing to the formation and maintenance of neural networks. It influences neuronal signaling and connectivity, which are essential for the proper functioning of the central nervous system (Lengyel2021Chromosome). CNTNAP5's expression is restricted to retinal and neural tissues, with significant enrichment in central retinal tissues, indicating its specialized role in these areas (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS). The gene's involvement in these molecular processes underscores its critical role in the development and function of the nervous system, with implications for various neurological and retinal diseases.

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and alterations in the CNTNAP5 gene have been implicated in several neurological and developmental disorders. Rare deletions in CNTNAP5 have been associated with autism spectrum disorders (ASD) and dyslexia. A specific 227 kb deletion in CNTNAP5, which removes exons 4 to 11, was identified in a family where the deletion was transmitted from the father to his autistic sons, suggesting a potential link between CNTNAP5 deletions and autism (Pagnamenta2010Characterization). Further analysis in ASD families identified nonsynonymous mutations in CNTNAP5, with some mutations predicted to be damaging, indicating that rare variants in CNTNAP5 may contribute to ASD (Pagnamenta2010Characterization).

CNTNAP5 has also been associated with primary angle-closure glaucoma (PACG), a major cause of blindness. Genetic studies have identified significant associations between SNPs in the CNTNAP5 locus and PACG, suggesting its involvement in glaucomatous neurodegeneration (Chakraborty2021Haplotypebased). Functional studies using zebrafish models have shown that knockdown of cntnap5 leads to retinal degeneration and visual deficits, supporting its role in retinal development and neurodegeneration (Chakraborty2024PostGWAS).

Additionally, CNTNAP5 has been linked to metabolic conditions, with some studies suggesting a role in obesity and metabolism (Lengyel2021Chromosome). These findings highlight the gene's potential involvement in a range of neurodevelopmental, neurological, and metabolic disorders.
