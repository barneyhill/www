# SNRPB2

## Overview
The SNRPB2 gene encodes the small nuclear ribonucleoprotein polypeptide B2, a critical component of the spliceosomal small nuclear ribonucleoproteins (snRNPs) involved in pre-mRNA splicing. This protein plays a vital role in the assembly and function of the spliceosome, a complex responsible for the precise removal of introns from pre-mRNA transcripts, a process essential for the generation of mature mRNA molecules. The SNRPB2 protein is characterized by its ability to interact with other snRNP components, facilitating the formation of a functional spliceosome complex. It undergoes various post-translational modifications, such as phosphorylation and methylation, which regulate its activity and interactions. Dysregulation or mutations in the SNRPB2 gene have been associated with several pathological conditions, including neurodegenerative disorders and cancers, highlighting its importance in maintaining normal cellular function and gene expression (Qiu2020aarFDC).

Alterations in the expression or function of SNRPB2 can disrupt normal RNA processing and gene expression regulation, leading to pathological conditions.

The precise mechanisms by which SNRPB2 mutations lead to these disorders are still under investigation, but the gene's role in RNA processing is a critical factor.

Abnormal splicing events may lead to the production of protein variants that enhance cell proliferation or survival, contributing to cancer development and progression. Understanding the specific alterations in SNRPB2 and their impact on splicing and gene expression is essential for developing targeted therapies for these conditions.
