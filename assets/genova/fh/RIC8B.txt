# RIC8B

## Overview
RIC8B is a gene that encodes the RIC8 guanine nucleotide exchange factor B, a protein that plays a pivotal role in the regulation of G-protein signaling. As a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF), the RIC8B protein facilitates the exchange of GDP for GTP on Gα subunits, which is a critical step in the activation of G proteins involved in various intracellular signaling pathways (Chan2011Ric8B). The protein is particularly significant in olfactory sensory neurons, where it enhances odorant receptor signaling, and is also implicated in the regulation of G protein biosynthesis and turnover (Nagai2019Depletion; Von2006Ric8B). Additionally, RIC8B has been associated with neurological disorders and cancer, highlighting its clinical significance in disease contexts (Solis2024Neomorphic; Wu2022CircRIC8B).

## Function
RIC8B functions as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for G protein alpha subunits, specifically Gαs, Gαolf, Gαq, and Gα13. It facilitates the exchange of GDP for GTP, a critical step in activating G proteins, which are involved in various intracellular signaling pathways (Chan2011Ric8B). In healthy human cells, RIC8B enhances the expression and stability of Gα subunits, particularly Gαolf, by promoting their localization to the plasma membrane, which is essential for effective signal transduction (Von2006Ric8B).

RIC8B is active in the cytoplasm and plays a significant role in olfactory sensory neurons, where it amplifies odorant receptor signaling through Gαolf. This function is crucial for the proper perception of odors (Von2006Ric8B). Additionally, RIC8B is involved in the regulation of G protein biosynthesis and turnover, potentially acting as a chaperone to stabilize Gα proteins and facilitate the formation of functional G protein complexes (Nagai2019Depletion). The protein's activity is GTP-dependent, with higher GTP concentrations enhancing its ability to activate Gαs (Chan2011Ric8B). RIC8B is also implicated in mTORC2 activity, which is important for cytoskeletal organization, cell survival, and migration (Nagai2019Depletion).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and altered expression of the RIC8B gene have been implicated in various neurological disorders. In the context of GNAO1 encephalopathies, RIC8B interacts with GNAO1 mutants, which are associated with developmental and epileptic encephalopathy-17 (DEE17) and neurodevelopmental disorder with involuntary movements (NEDIM). These interactions are linked to disease onset and clinical severity, suggesting that RIC8B's interaction with GNAO1 mutants could serve as a biomarker for these conditions (Solis2024Neomorphic).

The neomorphic interactions between RIC8B and GNAO1 mutants disrupt normal G protein signaling pathways, contributing to the dominance of these disorders. This disruption affects the guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) and chaperone activities of RIC8B, impacting other Gα subunits and potentially leading to severe neurological manifestations (Solis2024Neomorphic).

In chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), RIC8B is involved in lipid metabolism regulation through the miR-199b-5p/LPL axis. Its expression is associated with aggressive clinical features and poor prognosis in CLL patients, indicating its potential role in cancer progression (Wu2022CircRIC8B).

## Interactions
RIC8B, also known as RIC8 guanine nucleotide exchange factor B, interacts with various G protein alpha subunits, playing a crucial role in G protein signaling. It acts as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for Gαs, Gαolf, Gαq, and Gα13 subunits, facilitating the exchange of GDP for GTP, which is essential for G protein activation (Chan2011Ric8B). Ric-8BFL, the full-length version of Ric-8B, shows a strong binding preference for Gαs subunits, forming stable complexes in the presence of GDP and promoting nucleotide exchange (Chan2011Ric8B). 

Ric-8B also interacts with Gαq and Gα13, enhancing their GTPγS binding rates, although the truncated version, Ric-8BΔ9, does not affect these subunits (Chan2011Ric8B). The interaction with Gαs is particularly significant, as Ric-8BFL can modulate the GTPase activity of Gαs, acting as a GEF activator at physiological GTP concentrations (Chan2011Ric8B). 

In the context of GNAO1 encephalopathies, Ric8B exhibits neomorphic interactions with mutant Gαo proteins, which are not present in the wild-type. These interactions are linked to disease severity and suggest a potential biomarker role for Ric8B in assessing clinical manifestations (Solis2023Ric8; Solis2024Neomorphic).
