# GARIN2

## Overview
GARIN2, or Golgi Associated RAB2 Interactor Family Member 2, is a gene that encodes a protein involved in intracellular transport processes, specifically within the Golgi apparatus. The protein encoded by GARIN2 is integral to the trafficking of vesicles between the endoplasmic reticulum and the Golgi apparatus, a critical pathway for maintaining cellular function and homeostasis. As a Golgi-associated protein, GARIN2 interacts with RAB2, a small GTPase, to facilitate vesicle trafficking, ensuring the proper sorting and secretion of proteins within the cell. This interaction is essential for maintaining the structure and function of the Golgi apparatus, which is pivotal for processing and packaging proteins for secretion or delivery to other parts of the cell. The protein's role in vesicle trafficking impacts various cellular processes, including protein sorting and secretion, underscoring its importance in cellular communication and function. Disruptions in GARIN2 function can lead to cellular dysfunctions, highlighting its significance in maintaining cellular integrity and organismal health (Qiu2020aarFDC).
