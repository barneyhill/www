# ARFGEF3

## Overview
ARFGEF3, or ADP-ribosylation factor guanine nucleotide-exchange factor 3, is a gene that encodes a protein involved in critical cellular processes, particularly vesicle trafficking. The protein, known as ARFGEF family member 3, is characterized by the presence of Sec7 domains, which are essential for its function as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for ARF proteins. These ARF proteins are small GTPases that play a pivotal role in membrane dynamics and vesicle formation, primarily within the Golgi apparatus and endosomes. ARFGEF3 is implicated in the regulation of protein sorting and transport, processes vital for cellular homeostasis and function. The protein's interactions, including those with Rab GTPases like Rab2A, further underscore its regulatory role in membrane trafficking. Additionally, ARFGEF3 has been identified as a potential biomarker in cancer and is involved in stress response pathways, highlighting its significance in both normal cellular physiology and disease contexts (FigueroaMartínez2024Computational; Strekalova2023Molecular; Gillingham2019In).

## Interactions
ARFGEF3, also known as BIG3, is involved in interactions with Rab GTPases, particularly Rab2A. The MitoID method identified ARFGEF3 as a novel interaction partner of Rab2A-GTP, suggesting that Rab2A can recruit ARFGEF3 to membranes, indicating a regulatory role for Rab2A in the function of ARFGEF3 (Gillingham2019In). This interaction was validated through experiments using GST-Rab-coated beads and GFP-tagged proteins, which relocalized to mitochondria when co-expressed with mitochondrial Rab2A-GTP (Gillingham2019In).

ARFGEF3 is also highlighted as a potential biomarker in cancer, with significant overexpression noted in breast cancer cases and cell lines. However, specific details on its physical interactions with other proteins or nucleic acids in this context are not provided (FigueroaMartínez2024Computational). In the context of stress response, ARFGEF3 is upregulated in stressed mice with genetic inactivation of neuronal serotonin synthesis, but again, specific interaction details are not mentioned (Strekalova2023Molecular).

Overall, ARFGEF3's interactions primarily involve its role in membrane trafficking and its potential regulatory functions in cellular processes, particularly through its interaction with Rab2A.
