# CCDC171

## Overview
CCDC171 is a gene that encodes the protein coiled-coil domain containing 171, which is part of the coiled-coil domain-containing family. This family of proteins is known for its involvement in structural organization and intracellular transport, primarily through the formation of protein complexes that maintain cellular architecture and function (Gentile2016Functional). The CCDC171 protein is specifically expressed in ciliated cells within lung tissue, where it localizes to the membrane, suggesting a potential role in cilia-related functions (Lindskog2014The). Additionally, CCDC171 is a target gene of the transcription factor NRF1, which is involved in mitochondrial function and oxidative stress response, indicating a possible role in these cellular processes (French2016Germline). Despite these associations, the precise biological functions of CCDC171 in healthy human cells remain to be fully elucidated.

## Function
The CCDC171 gene encodes a protein that is part of the coiled-coil domain-containing family, which is involved in various cellular processes, including structural organization and intracellular transport. Proteins with coiled-coil domains are known for their role in forming protein complexes, which are crucial for maintaining cellular architecture and function (Gentile2016Functional). In the context of lung tissue, CCDC171 is specifically expressed in ciliated cells, where it is localized to the membrane. This suggests a potential role in cilia-related functions, although the specific molecular activities of CCDC171 in healthy human cells remain unclear (Lindskog2014The).

CCDC171 has been identified as a target gene of NRF1, a transcription factor involved in mitochondrial function and oxidative stress response, indicating a possible role in these processes (French2016Germline). Despite these associations, the precise biological functions and organismal outcomes of CCDC171's activity in healthy human cells are not well-characterized. The gene's involvement in cilia-related processes and its expression in specific cell types suggest it may contribute to maintaining cellular structure and function, particularly in tissues where cilia play a critical role (Gentile2016Functional).

## Clinical Significance
CCDC171 has been implicated in several diseases and conditions through various studies. In epithelial ovarian cancer (EOC), CCDC171 is associated with high-grade serous EOC risk. A transcriptome-wide association study identified CCDC171 as a gene with genetically predicted expression linked to EOC risk, suggesting it may be a novel risk locus for this disease (Lu2018A). Additionally, high levels of CCDC171 expression have been associated with progression-free survival in EOC patients with nil residual disease, although this association is not evident in patients with any debulking status (French2016Germline).

In bipolar disorder, CCDC171 was identified as one of the top hits for rare variants in a whole-exome sequencing study. The analysis showed a higher proportion of bipolar disorder cases carrying variants in CCDC171 compared to controls, although the association did not reach exome-wide significance (Husson2018Identification).

CCDC171 has also been linked to body mass index (BMI) through gene-diet interactions. A study in the Singapore Chinese population found that cholesterol intake interacts with a SNP in CCDC171, potentially influencing BMI levels (Chang2018Genediet). These findings suggest that CCDC171 may play a role in various conditions through its expression levels and genetic variations.
