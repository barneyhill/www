# EPGN

## Overview
The EPGN gene encodes the protein epithelial mitogen, a member of the epidermal growth factor (EGF) family, which is integral to cellular signaling and growth processes, particularly in epithelial tissues. As a growth factor, the EPGN protein is involved in promoting the proliferation and differentiation of epithelial cells, playing a pivotal role in tissue maintenance and repair. This protein's activity is crucial for processes such as wound healing and tissue regeneration, where it binds to specific cell surface receptors to initiate signaling pathways that regulate cell division and differentiation. The structural complexity of EPGN, including its primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structures, as well as post-translational modifications, underpins its functional versatility and regulatory capacity within cellular environments.
