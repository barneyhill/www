# FAM222A

## Overview
FAM222A is a gene that encodes the protein known as Aggregatin, which is implicated in the pathogenesis of Alzheimer's disease (AD). Aggregatin belongs to a family of proteins characterized by sequence similarity, specifically the family with sequence similarity 222 member A. This protein is predominantly expressed in the central nervous system and plays a crucial role in the aggregation of amyloid-beta (Aβ) peptides, a process central to the formation of amyloid plaques, a hallmark of AD pathology (Yan2020FAM222A). Aggregatin interacts with Aβ through its N-terminal Aβ binding domain, acting as a seeding factor for Aβ oligomerization and aggregation (Yan2020FAM222A). The gene's involvement in AD, along with its specific interaction with Aβ, underscores its potential as a target for therapeutic intervention in neurodegenerative diseases (Yan2020FAM222A).

## Clinical Significance
The FAM222A gene encodes a protein known as Aggregatin, which is implicated in Alzheimer's disease (AD). Alterations in the expression of FAM222A are associated with the accumulation of Aggregatin in amyloid plaques, a hallmark of AD pathology. This protein interacts with amyloid-beta (Aβ) via its N-terminal Aβ binding domain, facilitating Aβ aggregation and contributing to plaque formation. Increased levels of Aggregatin exacerbate neuroinflammation and cognitive dysfunction in AD models, while its ablation reduces amyloid deposits and associated pathologies, suggesting its pathological relevance in AD (Yan2020FAM222A).

A specific single nucleotide polymorphism (SNP) in FAM222A, rs117028417, has been linked to brain atrophy in regions affected by AD, such as the hippocampus and entorhinal area. This SNP is associated with changes in brain volume and amyloid deposition, although its direct association with AD diagnosis is marginal (Yan2020FAM222A). Genetic studies also suggest potential interactions between FAM222A and established AD risk genes, such as APOE, TOMM40, and APOC1, although a strong influence of FAM222A variants on AD risk has not been observed (Yan2020FAM222A). These findings highlight the potential of FAM222A as a target for therapeutic intervention in Alzheimer's disease.

## Interactions
The FAM222A gene encodes a protein known as Aggregatin, which is involved in Alzheimer's disease (AD) through its interactions with amyloid-beta (Aβ) peptides. Aggregatin physically interacts with Aβ via its N-terminal Aβ binding domain (NABD), facilitating Aβ aggregation, a key process in amyloid plaque formation, a hallmark of AD (Satoh2020Reactive; Yan2020FAM222A). This interaction has been confirmed through in vitro pull-down assays, which demonstrated that recombinant Aggregatin can co-precipitate with different forms of Aβ, specifically Aβ 1-40 and Aβ 1-42 (Yan2020FAM222A). Surface plasmon resonance (SPR) measurements have shown that Aβ binds to Aggregatin with low nanomolar dissociation equilibrium constants, indicating a strong interaction (Yan2020FAM222A).

Aggregatin acts as a seeding factor for Aβ oligomerization and aggregation, promoting the formation of amyloid plaques (Yan2020FAM222A). Despite its significant role in Aβ aggregation, Aggregatin does not physically interact with other proteins such as tau, α-synuclein, or APOE, suggesting a specific link with Aβ (Yan2020FAM222A). The protein is predominantly expressed in the central nervous system and is found in amyloid plaques in AD patients and mouse models (Yan2020FAM222A).
