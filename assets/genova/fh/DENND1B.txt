# DENND1B

## Overview
The DENND1B gene encodes the DENN domain-containing 1B protein, which functions as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) primarily involved in endosomal trafficking. This protein is crucial for the regulation of T cell receptor (TCR) signaling in Th2 cells, playing a significant role in immune response modulation. The DENND1B protein, characterized by its DENN domain, facilitates the exchange of GDP for GTP on Rab35, a member of the Rab GTPase family, thereby influencing vesicle transport and membrane trafficking. Its activity is essential for maintaining immune system homeostasis and preventing excessive cytokine production, which is associated with conditions such as atopic asthma (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms). Additionally, the DENND1B gene has been linked to various allergic and inflammatory conditions, as well as certain cancers, highlighting its clinical significance (Chen2022circRNA).

## Function
The DENND1B gene encodes a protein that functions as a guanine nucleotide exchange factor (GEF) for Rab35, a member of the Rab GTPase family involved in vesicle transport and membrane trafficking. In healthy human cells, DENND1B plays a crucial role in the regulation of T cell receptor (TCR) down-regulation on Th2 cells. This regulation is essential for modulating the duration of TCR signaling, which in turn controls cytokine production by Th2 cells. Proper functioning of DENND1B ensures that TCR molecules, once activated by an antigen, are efficiently targeted to the immunological synapse and subsequently directed to endosomes for degradation or recycling. This process is vital for maintaining normal immune responses and preventing excessive Th2 cytokine production, which is associated with conditions such as atopic asthma (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms).

The DENND1B protein is active in the cytoplasm, where it contributes to immune response regulation and cellular communication. Its role in modulating TCR signaling and cytokine production highlights its importance in immune system homeostasis and the prevention of immune-related disorders (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and polymorphisms in the DENND1B gene have been associated with various allergic and inflammatory conditions, particularly asthma and atopy. The DENND1B gene plays a crucial role in regulating T cell receptor (TCR) down-regulation on Th2 cells, which is important for controlling cytokine production. Alterations in this gene can lead to increased Th2 responses, which are linked to asthma, especially atopic asthma (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms). 

A study involving Brazilian children identified several single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) in the DENND1B gene that are associated with asthma and atopy phenotypes. For instance, the rs6691216 (C) allele was found to be a protective factor against asthma, while alleles such as rs1421396 (A) and rs1421389 (C) were identified as risk factors, associated with increased IL-13 production (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms). These genetic variations may influence the expression of DENND1B, affecting immune responses and contributing to the pathophysiology of asthma and atopy (Fiuza2017Polymorphisms).

In addition to its role in asthma, the DENND1B gene has been implicated in cancer. The circular RNA circ_DENND1B is downregulated in clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC) and acts as a tumor suppressor by inhibiting miR-122-5p, which regulates the expression of TIMP2, a tumor suppressor gene (Chen2022circRNA). Overexpression of circ_DENND1B in ccRCC cells reduces proliferation, migration, and invasion, highlighting its potential as a therapeutic target (Chen2022circRNA).
