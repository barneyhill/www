# KRTAP6-1

## Overview
KRTAP6-1 is a gene that encodes the keratin-associated protein 6-1, a member of the high glycine-tyrosine (HGT) group of keratin-associated proteins. These proteins play a crucial role in the structural integrity and function of hair and other keratinized tissues. The KRTAP6-1 protein is characterized by its high glycine and tyrosine content, which contributes to its ability to interact with keratin intermediate filaments, forming part of the matrix that supports hair structure. This interaction is facilitated by the protein's repetitive glycine-tyrosine sequences and its capacity to form disulfide bonds, which enhance the strength and resilience of hair fibers (Rogers2002Characterization; Rogers2006Human). The gene is primarily expressed in hair follicle cells, where it influences hair traits such as fiber strength and flexibility (Rogers2006Human).

## Structure
The KRTAP6-1 protein is characterized by its high glycine and tyrosine content, which ranges from 54.6% to 60.5% (Rogers2002Characterization). The primary structure of KRTAP6-1 includes repetitive units of glycine-tyrosine and glycine-tyrosine-glycine residues, which are a defining feature of the KAP6 family (Rogers2006Human). These repetitive sequences contribute to the protein's molecular structure and function.

## Function
KRTAP6-1 is a member of the keratin-associated protein family, specifically classified within the high glycine-tyrosine (HGT) group. These proteins are integral to the structure and function of hair, interacting with keratin intermediate filaments to form the hair shaft and other keratinized tissues. The KRTAP6 family, including KRTAP6-1, is characterized by a high content of glycine and tyrosine, which contributes to the formation and stabilization of hair and other keratinous structures (Rogers2006Human).

In humans, KRTAP6-1 is involved in the molecular processes that underpin the structural framework of hair fibers. The protein's small size and specific amino acid composition, including significant amounts of cysteine and serine, suggest its role in forming disulfide bonds with hair keratins, enhancing the strength and resilience of hair fibers (Rogers2006Human). The central portions of these proteins consist of repetitive units of glycine-tyrosine and glycine-tyrosine-glycine residues, which are crucial for their interaction with keratin filaments (Rogers2006Human).

KRTAP6-1 is primarily active in hair follicle cells, where it contributes to the development and maintenance of hair structure, influencing traits such as fiber strength and flexibility (Rogers2006Human).
