# THAP6

## Overview
THAP6 is a gene that encodes the THAP domain containing 6 protein, which is a member of the human THAP protein family. This family is characterized by the presence of a conserved THAP domain, a zinc-coordinating DNA-binding motif, which suggests a role in transcriptional regulation. The THAP6 protein is classified as a short THAP protein (sTHAP) due to its length of less than 250 amino acids. It is primarily involved in transcriptional regulation within the nucleus and is implicated in cellular processes such as apoptosis and cell proliferation, highlighting its importance in maintaining cellular homeostasis. The protein's structure includes a predicted alpha helical region that may facilitate oligomerization, allowing it to form both homo- and hetero-oligomers, which could influence its functional roles in cellular processes (Sanghavi2019Classification).

## Structure
THAP6 is a member of the human THAP protein family, characterized by a conserved amino-terminal THAP domain, which is a zinc-coordinating DNA-binding domain. The THAP domain in THAP6 is 48 residues long and includes a C2CH zinc-coordinating motif, although some homologs lack certain cysteine residues in this motif (Sanghavi2021Synapomorphic). The primary structure of THAP6 is less than 250 amino acids long, classifying it as a short THAP protein (sTHAP) (Sanghavi2019Classification).

The secondary structure of THAP6 includes a predicted alpha helical region spanning residues 148-193, which is highly conserved and rich in leucine and hydrophobic residues, indicative of a coiled coil region (Sanghavi2019Classification). This region is important for potential oligomerization, as it may form higher order structures such as dimers or tetramers (Sanghavi2019Classification).

The tertiary structure predictions for THAP6 suggest a compact model, with the alpha helical regions overlapping with those identified by secondary structure prediction tools, indicating structural similarity within the sTHAP group (Sanghavi2019Classification). The quaternary structure of THAP6 is suggested to involve the formation of both homo- and hetero-oligomers, facilitated by specific amino acid interactions within the alpha helical regions (Sanghavi2019Classification).

## Function
THAP6 (THAP domain containing 6) is a gene that encodes a protein involved in transcriptional regulation, primarily active in the nucleus. The THAP domain is a zinc finger-like motif that facilitates DNA binding, suggesting a role in modulating gene expression. This protein is implicated in cellular processes such as apoptosis and cell proliferation, indicating its importance in maintaining cellular homeostasis and function (Sanghavi2019Classification).

The THAP6 protein is part of the sTHAP group, characterized by a short length of less than 250 amino acids and strong evolutionary conservation in its alpha helical regions. These regions are predicted to form coiled coil structures, which are crucial for protein oligomerization. This structural feature suggests that THAP6 may participate in forming both homo- and hetero-oligomers, potentially influencing its role in cellular processes (Sanghavi2019Classification).

Despite the detailed structural analysis, specific functions of THAP6 in healthy human cells remain less defined. However, its involvement in transcriptional regulation and potential interactions through oligomerization highlight its significance in cellular and molecular biology (Sanghavi2019Classification).

## Interactions
THAP6, a member of the human THAP protein family, is predicted to participate in protein-protein interactions through its alpha helical regions, which have a high probability of forming coiled coil structures. These regions are rich in leucine and hydrophobic residues, characteristic of coiled coils, and span residues 148-193 in THAP6 (Sanghavi2019Classification). The potential for oligomerization, both homo- and hetero-oligomerization, is suggested, although specific interaction partners for THAP6 are not detailed (Sanghavi2019Classification).

The predicted alpha helical regions of THAP6 show very little amphipathic arrangement, but specific electrostatic interactions are predicted within the sTHAP protein group, which includes THAP6. These interactions involve residues such as Arg161 and Lys166, and Arg175 and Glu176, which may contribute to the protein's structural stability and potential for oligomerization (Sanghavi2019Classification).

While the STRING database is mentioned as a source for identifying interacting partners, no specific interactions for THAP6 are provided in the available context (Sanghavi2019Classification). Therefore, detailed interaction partners or complexes involving THAP6 remain to be elucidated.
