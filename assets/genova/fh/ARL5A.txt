# ARL5A

## Overview
ARL5A (ADP ribosylation factor like GTPase 5A) is a gene that encodes a member of the ARF family of GTP-binding proteins, which are integral to intracellular trafficking and membrane dynamics. The protein product of ARL5A, also referred to as ARL5A, is involved in the recruitment of the Golgi-associated retrograde protein (GARP) complex to the Golgi apparatus, a process essential for endosome-to-Golgi traffic and cellular homeostasis (RosaFerreira2015The). As a GTPase, ARL5A plays a critical role in vesicular trafficking, organelle structure, and potentially chromatin organization through its interactions with various proteins, including heterochromatin protein 1 alpha (HP1α) and importin-α (Lin2002A). The protein's function is conserved across eukaryotes, underscoring its importance in cellular processes (RosaFerreira2015The).

## Function
ARL5A, a member of the ARF family of GTP-binding proteins, plays a significant role in intracellular trafficking and membrane dynamics. It is involved in the recruitment of the Golgi-associated retrograde protein (GARP) complex to the Golgi apparatus, which is crucial for endosome-to-Golgi traffic. This process is essential for maintaining cellular homeostasis and proper protein sorting (RosaFerreira2015The). ARL5A, along with its paralogue ARL5B, localizes to the trans-Golgi network and is implicated in the retrograde trafficking of vesicles from endosomes to the Golgi. This function is conserved across eukaryotes, highlighting its importance in cellular processes (RosaFerreira2015The).

In mammalian cells, ARL5A can substitute for ARL5B in promoting the recruitment of Vps54, a component of the GARP complex, to the trans-Golgi network when overexpressed. This suggests that ARL5A and ARL5B have overlapping roles in directing GARP recruitment, although ARL5B is more consistently expressed across major tissues (RosaFerreira2015The). The proper localization of the GARP complex facilitated by ARL5A is vital for efficient endosome-to-Golgi traffic, which is necessary for cellular recycling and secretion processes (RosaFerreira2015The).

## Interactions
ARL5A, a member of the ARF family of GTP-binding proteins, is involved in various protein interactions that are crucial for its function in vesicular trafficking and organelle structure. One of the key interactions of ARL5A is with heterochromatin protein 1 alpha (HP1α). This interaction is dependent on the GTP-bound state of ARL5A and requires a MIR-like motif at its C-terminus. The interaction with HP1α suggests a role for ARL5A in chromatin organization and nuclear architecture (Lin2002A).

ARL5A also interacts with importin-α, a nuclear transport receptor, through a bipartite nuclear localization signal (NLS) at its C-terminus. This interaction is essential for the nuclear import of ARL5A, as demonstrated by the lack of interaction in mutants lacking the NLS (Lin2002A).

In the context of Golgi apparatus function, ARL5A is involved in the recruitment of the GARP complex to the trans-Golgi network (TGN). It physically interacts with the GARP complex, which is essential for tethering vesicles at the TGN. This interaction is crucial for the localization of the GARP complex and is regulated by ARFRP1, which functions upstream of ARL5A (RosaFerreira2015The; Ishida2019ARFRP1).
