# ATP4B

## Overview
The ATP4B gene encodes the beta subunit of the gastric H+/K+ ATPase, a transmembrane protein complex essential for acid secretion in the stomach. This protein plays a critical role in maintaining the acidic environment necessary for digestion and pathogen defense by facilitating the exchange of hydrogen and potassium ions in gastric parietal cells (Zhao2010Atp4b; Lahner2017Luminescent). The beta subunit, encoded by ATP4B, is crucial for the stability and proper functioning of the alpha subunit, which contains the catalytic site for ion transport. Dysregulation of ATP4B expression has been implicated in gastric cancer, where its downregulation is associated with poor prognosis and advanced disease stages (Pan2020ATP4B; Li2020ITRAQbased). Understanding the structure and function of ATP4B is vital for exploring its potential as a therapeutic target and its role in gastric pathophysiology.

In the quaternary structure, ATP4B assembles with the alpha subunit of the H+/K+ ATPase, forming a functional complex essential for its activity in gastric acid secretion. ## Function
The ATP4B gene encodes the beta subunit of the gastric H+/K+ ATPase, a crucial enzyme located in the parietal cells of the gastric oxyntic mucosa. This enzyme is responsible for maintaining the acidic environment of the stomach by exchanging intracellular hydrogen ions (H+) for extracellular potassium ions (K+), a process essential for digestion and defense against pathogens (Zhao2010Atp4b; Lahner2017Luminescent). The beta subunit, encoded by ATP4B, plays a vital role in stabilizing the alpha subunit, which contains the catalytic site necessary for ion transport (Lahner2017Luminescent).

In healthy human cells, the ATP4B protein is primarily active in the parietal cells of the stomach lining, where it supports the function of the gastric proton pump. This pump utilizes energy derived from ATP hydrolysis to transport ions against their concentration gradients, thereby facilitating gastric acid secretion (Lahner2017Luminescent). The proper function of this enzyme is critical for the digestive process, as it helps break down food and absorb nutrients. Additionally, the acidic environment it creates serves as a barrier to pathogens, contributing to the body's immune defense (Zhao2010Atp4b).

## Clinical Significance
The ATP4B gene, encoding the beta subunit of the gastric H+/K+ ATPase, is clinically significant in the context of gastric cancer (GC). In GC, ATP4B expression is notably downregulated, which is associated with poor prognosis and advanced disease stages (Pan2020ATP4B; Li2020ITRAQbased). This downregulation is primarily due to epigenetic alterations, including DNA hypermethylation and histone hypoacetylation, leading to its silencing in gastric cancer cells (Lin2017Silencing). The silencing of ATP4B contributes to tumor progression by affecting pathways related to cell proliferation, migration, and apoptosis, particularly through the mitochondrial/p53/NF-κB pathway (Pan2020ATP4B; Li2020ITRAQbased).

Restoration of ATP4B expression in GC cells has been shown to inhibit tumor growth and enhance the efficacy of chemotherapeutic agents like docetaxel, suggesting its potential as a therapeutic target (Pan2020ATP4B; Lin2017Silencing). Additionally, ATP4B is linked to autoimmune atrophic gastritis, where autoantibodies against ATP4B serve as diagnostic markers, indicating an increased risk for gastric cancer (Li2020ITRAQbased). These findings underscore the gene's role as a tumor suppressor and its potential utility in cancer diagnosis and treatment.
