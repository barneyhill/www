# HES7

## Overview
HES7 is a gene that encodes the hes family bHLH transcription factor 7, a member of the basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) superfamily of transcription factors. This protein plays a pivotal role in the segmentation clock, a molecular oscillator crucial for the periodic formation of somites during vertebrate embryogenesis. As a transcriptional repressor, HES7 is involved in a negative feedback loop that regulates its own expression and that of the Lunatic fringe (Lfng) gene, which is part of the Notch signaling pathway. The precise oscillatory expression of HES7, regulated by pathways such as Notch and WNT, is essential for the correct timing and formation of somites, which are precursors to vertebrae and ribs (Chu2019An; Bessho2003Periodic). Mutations in the HES7 gene are linked to spondylocostal dysostosis, a disorder characterized by abnormal vertebral segmentation, highlighting its clinical significance in skeletal development (Willet2015Canine).

## Structure
The HES7 protein is a member of the basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) superfamily of transcription factors, characterized by its involvement in transcriptional repression. The primary structure of HES7 includes a basic domain necessary for DNA binding to N-box sequences (5'-CACNAG-3') and a helix-loop-helix (HLH) domain required for homo- and heterodimerization with other bHLH proteins (Trofka2012The; Sparrow2010Two). The protein also contains a conserved Orange domain immediately carboxy-terminal to the bHLH domain, which influences dimer specificity, and a carboxy-terminal tetrapeptide (WRPW) that mediates repression of target genes by recruiting the corepressor Groucho to DNA (Trofka2012The; Sparrow2010Two).

HES7 functions as a transcriptional repressor, capable of repressing its own promoter and that of other genes, such as Lfng, through specific binding sites, including RBP-J, E-boxes, and N-boxes (Bessho2003Periodic). The protein is rapidly degraded by the ubiquitin-proteasome system, which supports its role in a 2-hour oscillation cycle essential for somite segmentation (Bessho2003Periodic). ## Function
The HES7 gene encodes a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor that plays a critical role in the segmentation clock, a molecular oscillator essential for the periodic formation of somites during vertebrate embryogenesis. HES7 functions as a transcriptional repressor, forming a negative feedback loop by inhibiting its own transcription as well as that of the Lunatic fringe (Lfng) gene, which is involved in the Notch signaling pathway (Bessho2003Periodic). This repression is crucial for the cyclic gene expression necessary for the proper timing and formation of somites, which are precursors to vertebrae and ribs (Chu2019An; Bessho2003Periodic).

In healthy human cells, HES7's oscillatory expression is regulated by signaling pathways, including the Notch and WNT pathways, which are essential for maintaining the precision of the segmentation clock (Chu2019An). The protein is rapidly degraded by the ubiquitin-proteasome system, with a half-life of approximately 23.1 minutes, allowing for its dynamic expression in the presomitic mesoderm (PSM) (Bessho2003Periodic). This precise regulation ensures the correct segmentation of somites, which is vital for normal vertebral development (Fujimuro2014Hes7).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations in the HES7 gene are associated with spondylocostal dysostosis (SCD), a disorder characterized by abnormal vertebral segmentation. In humans, various mutations in HES7 can lead to different severities of SCD, with some causing minor motor and growth retardation and others resulting in severe defects and infant mortality (Willet2015Canine). Specifically, missense mutations such as I58V and D186Y have been identified in individuals with SCD, where these mutations disrupt the gene's function in the Notch signaling pathway, crucial for vertebral segmentation (Sparrow2010Two). 

A novel homozygous splicing variant, c.226+1G>A, has also been linked to spondylocostal dysostosis 4 (SCDO4), causing symptoms like short stature, rib and spine deformities, and restrictive ventilatory defects (Lv2023A). The clinical significance of HES7 mutations is underscored by their impact on skeletal development, with mutations leading to impaired transcriptional repression and somitogenesis defects (Willet2015Canine). Additionally, a 3′UTR variation in HES7 has been associated with dextrocardia, suggesting a broader impact of HES7 mutations on developmental processes (Netravathi2015Whole).

## Interactions
HES7, a basic helix-loop-helix (bHLH) transcription factor, is involved in several interactions that are crucial for its function in the segmentation clock during somitogenesis. HES7 protein is known to bind specifically to its own promoter region, as well as the promoter region of the Lunatic fringe (Lfng) gene, indicating its role in transcriptional regulation through direct DNA interactions (Bessho2003Periodic). This binding is part of a negative feedback loop that regulates the cyclic expression of these genes, essential for the segmentation clock (Bessho2003Periodic).

HES7 also interacts with various signaling pathways, including Notch, Fgf, and Wnt, which are crucial for its expression in the presomitic mesoderm (PSM) (Hayashi2018Presomitic). The protein can repress transcription via E-boxes, which are critical for the cyclic expression of Lfng, and it can override Notch-induced transcription from its promoter (Bessho2003Periodic). HES7's interaction with the Notch signaling pathway is further highlighted by its ability to influence the oscillatory expression of Notch modulator Lfng, which in turn affects Notch signaling (Harima2013Oscillatory). These interactions underscore HES7's role in maintaining the precise timing and coordination necessary for proper somite segmentation.
