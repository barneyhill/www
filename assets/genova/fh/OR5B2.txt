# OR5B2

## Overview
OR5B2 is a gene that encodes the protein olfactory receptor family 5 subfamily B member 2, which is part of the extensive family of olfactory receptors. These receptors are a subset of G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), known for their seven-transmembrane domain architecture, which is integral to their role in detecting odorant molecules and initiating olfactory signal transduction (Spielman2013Membrane). The OR5B2 protein, like other olfactory receptors, is involved in the molecular mechanisms underlying the sense of smell, where it binds specific ligands and triggers a cascade of cellular responses (cvicek2015structure). This receptor's structure and function are typical of GPCRs, which are pivotal in various physiological processes due to their ability to transduce extracellular signals into intracellular responses (cvicek2015structure).

## Structure
