# GTF2IRD2B

## Overview
GTF2IRD2B is a gene that encodes the protein GTF2I repeat domain containing 2B, which is involved in transcriptional regulation. This protein is characterized by the presence of GTF2I-like repeats, which are essential for its ability to bind DNA and interact with other proteins. These features enable the GTF2IRD2B protein to modulate gene expression, influencing various cellular processes such as development and differentiation. The protein is primarily localized in the nucleus, where it plays a significant role in transcriptional networks, contributing to the regulation of specific genes necessary for maintaining normal physiological conditions. Although the detailed mechanisms of its action remain to be fully elucidated, the GTF2IRD2B protein's role in transcriptional regulation underscores its importance in cellular homeostasis and organismal development (Qiu2020aarFDC).
