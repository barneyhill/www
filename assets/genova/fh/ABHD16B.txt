# ABHD16B

## Overview
ABHD16B is a gene that encodes the protein abhydrolase domain containing 16B, which is a member of the abhydrolase domain family. This family of proteins is characterized by their enzymatic activity in lipid metabolism, specifically functioning as hydrolases that facilitate the breakdown of lipid substrates. The ABHD16B protein plays a crucial role in maintaining cellular lipid homeostasis, which is essential for various cellular processes, including signal transduction and membrane dynamics. These processes are vital for cellular health, influencing how cells respond to external stimuli and manage membrane composition and fluidity. Although the precise substrates and pathways involving ABHD16B are not fully understood, its involvement in lipid metabolism highlights its importance in cellular and organismal health. The gene has been studied in the context of male infertility, particularly in cattle, where mutations can affect sperm maturation and function (Shan2020Association; Shan2024Identification). Additionally, ABHD16B has been linked to aberrant methylation patterns in infertile men, suggesting a broader role in reproductive health (Shan2024Identification).

## Clinical Significance
The ABHD16B gene has been implicated in male infertility, particularly in cattle, where it is associated with bull infertility due to its role in lipid metabolism and sperm maturation. A nonsense variant in ABHD16B results in a premature stop codon, truncating the protein and potentially affecting its function, which has been linked to infertility in Holstein cattle (Shan2020Association; Shan2024Identification). This variant leads to significant changes in sperm lipid composition, such as decreased sphingomyelin and diacylglycerols, and increased phosphatidylcholine and ceramide, which may impair sperm membrane integrity and function (Shan2020Association; Shan2024Identification).

In humans, ABHD16B has been associated with aberrant methylation patterns in infertile men, suggesting a potential link to male infertility (Shan2024Identification). The gene's involvement in lipid biosynthesis and its expression in testicular and epididymal tissues indicate its role in spermatogenesis and sperm maturation, which are critical for fertility (Shan2020Association; Shan2024Identification). While specific diseases directly linked to ABHD16B mutations in humans are not detailed, its role in lipid metabolism suggests potential implications for metabolic disorders (Shan2024Identification).
