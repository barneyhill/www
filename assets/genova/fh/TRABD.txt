# TRABD

## Overview
The TRABD gene encodes the TraB domain-containing protein, which is involved in various cellular processes, including signaling pathways and molecular interactions. The protein is characterized by the presence of the TraB domain, which is essential for its functional role in the cell. This domain facilitates the protein's interactions with other cellular components, potentially influencing pathways related to cellular growth and differentiation. The TRABD protein's activity can be modulated by post-translational modifications, such as phosphorylation, which may alter its interactions and functional outcomes. Additionally, the existence of splice variant isoforms of the TRABD gene contributes to the protein's structural and functional diversity, allowing it to adapt to different cellular contexts and perform a range of roles (Qiu2020aarFDC).

## Structure

This domain may influence the protein's ability to interact with other cellular components and participate in signaling pathways. Post-translational modifications, such as phosphorylation, can further modulate the protein's activity and interactions, potentially affecting its role in cellular processes.

Splice variant isoforms of the TRABD gene can lead to structural and functional diversity, allowing the protein to perform different roles within the cell. These isoforms may vary in their domain composition and post-translational modifications, contributing to the complexity of the protein's function.
