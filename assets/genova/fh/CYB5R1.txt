# CYB5R1

## Overview
CYB5R1 is a gene that encodes the enzyme cytochrome b5 reductase 1, which is a crucial component in electron transfer processes within cells. This enzyme is categorized as a reductase and is primarily involved in the desaturation and elongation of fatty acids, cholesterol biosynthesis, and drug metabolism. It is predominantly located in the endoplasmic reticulum and mitochondria, where it facilitates the reduction of cytochrome b5, thereby influencing cellular redox states and metabolic conversions (MartinMontalvo2016Cytochrome). The protein's role extends to the regulation of ferroptosis, a form of programmed cell death, by contributing to lipid peroxidation and membrane damage through the generation of reactive oxygen species (Yan2021Membrane). CYB5R1's involvement in these critical cellular processes underscores its importance in maintaining cellular homeostasis and its potential implications in various diseases, including cancer and neurodegenerative disorders (Yan2021Membrane; Woischke2016CYB5R1).

## Function
CYB5R1 (cytochrome b5 reductase 1) is an enzyme involved in electron transfer processes crucial for various metabolic pathways. It plays a significant role in the desaturation and elongation of fatty acids, cholesterol biosynthesis, and drug metabolism. The enzyme is typically located in the endoplasmic reticulum and mitochondria, where it facilitates the reduction of cytochrome b5, impacting cellular redox states and metabolic conversions (MartinMontalvo2016Cytochrome).

In the context of ferroptosis, a form of regulated cell death, CYB5R1 contributes to the production of hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) when incubated with NADH and oxygen. This H2O2 is crucial for generating hydroxyl radicals through the Fenton reaction, which initiates lipid peroxidation, leading to cell death (Yan2021Membrane). The enzyme's electron transfer ability is essential for catalyzing the oxidation of phospholipids, which causes membrane damage during ferroptosis (Yan2021Membrane).

While the specific function of CYB5R1 in healthy human cells is not directly addressed in the context, its involvement in electron transfer and lipid oxidation processes suggests a role in maintaining cellular homeostasis and membrane integrity (Yan2021Membrane).

## Clinical Significance
CYB5R1 (cytochrome b5 reductase 1) is implicated in several diseases and conditions due to its role in cellular redox balance and electron transfer processes. In colorectal cancer (CRC), high expression of CYB5R1 is associated with epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT), a process linked to cancer progression and poor prognosis. Elevated levels of CYB5R1 correlate with increased migration and invasion of colon cancer cells, making it a potential biomarker for poor prognosis and a target for therapeutic strategies in CRC (Woischke2016CYB5R1).

In the context of ferroptosis, a form of regulated cell death, CYB5R1 contributes to lipid peroxidation and membrane damage. It plays a role in generating reactive oxygen species, such as hydrogen peroxide, which are crucial for the ferroptosis process. This involvement suggests a potential link to diseases where ferroptosis is implicated, such as neurodegenerative diseases and certain cancers (Yan2021Membrane).

In gastric cancer, decreased CYB5R1 expression is associated with extended survival rates, as it promotes normal cellular energy metabolism and slows the progression from precancerous lesions to cancer (Kuang2023Identification).
