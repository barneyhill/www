# ABHD8

## Overview
ABHD8 is a gene that encodes the protein abhydrolase domain containing 8, which is part of the alpha/beta hydrolase fold family. This protein is characterized by its hydrolase activity, which involves the catalysis of the hydrolysis of chemical bonds. The ABHD8 protein is implicated in various cellular processes, including lipid metabolism and signal transduction, although its precise biological functions remain to be fully elucidated. Notably, ABHD8 has been associated with cancer biology, particularly in breast and ovarian cancers, where its expression levels have been linked to cancer susceptibility and progression (Lawrenson2016Functional). The gene's involvement in cancer is further supported by its interaction with specific single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) that influence its promoter activity, thereby affecting cancer risk (Lawrenson2016Functional).

## Clinical Significance
ABHD8 has been implicated in breast and ovarian cancer susceptibility, particularly in the context of the 19p13.1 locus. Studies have shown that alterations in ABHD8 expression are associated with increased cancer risk. Specifically, increased expression of ABHD8 is linked to an elevated risk of breast and ovarian cancer, with significant expression quantitative trait locus (eQTL) associations identified in ovarian cancers and normal breast tissues (Lawrenson2016Functional). 

Functional analyses suggest that certain single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) at the 19p13.1 locus enhance ABHD8 promoter activity, potentially increasing cancer risk through this mechanism (Lawrenson2016Functional). Overexpression of ABHD8 in breast and ovarian epithelial cells has been shown to significantly alter cell migration and invasion, which are critical processes in cancer metastasis (Lawrenson2016Functional). 

The gene is also part of a network associated with serous ovarian cancer risk, and its overexpression has been linked to changes in cellular movement and mTOR effects on ANKLE1 promoter activity (Lawrenson2016Functional). These findings suggest that ABHD8 is a novel susceptibility gene for breast and ovarian cancers, with functional SNPs at the 19p13.1 locus playing a role in its altered expression and associated cancer risks.
