# KRTAP10-7

## Overview
KRTAP10-7 is a gene that encodes the keratin associated protein 10-7, a member of the keratin-associated protein family. This family of proteins is integral to the structural integrity of keratinized tissues, such as hair and nails. The protein encoded by KRTAP10-7 is characterized by its high cysteine content, which facilitates the formation of disulfide bonds, crucial for the protein's stability and function. These structural features enable keratin associated protein 10-7 to interact with keratin intermediate filaments, contributing to the mechanical strength and resilience of hair fibers. Additionally, KRTAP10-7 is involved in the keratinization pathway, playing a significant role in epithelial tissue integrity by forming intermediate filaments that support cellular structures (Jaskiewicz2023Sequence). Beyond its structural role, the protein has been implicated in interactions with GSTP1, suggesting a potential involvement in skin homeostasis and response to oxidative stress (Bartolini2016The).

## Function
KRTAP10-7 (keratin associated protein 10-7) is part of the keratin-associated protein family, which plays a crucial role in the structural integrity of epithelial tissues. This protein is involved in the 'keratinization' pathway, contributing to the formation of intermediate filaments that provide mechanical support to cells. These filaments are essential for forming desmosomes between cells and hemi-desmosomes with basement membranes, ensuring epithelial integrity (Jaskiewicz2023Sequence). KRTAP10-7 is primarily active in the hair cortex, where it interacts with keratin intermediate filaments to provide mechanical strength and resilience to hair fibers, contributing to their formation and integrity.

In addition to its structural role, KRTAP10-7 has been identified in protein-protein interactions with GSTP1, a protein involved in oxidative stress responses. Although the specific molecular features of these interactions are not fully disclosed, they are speculated to be important for skin homeostasis, independent of GSTP1's detoxification function (Bartolini2016The). These interactions suggest that KRTAP10-7 may have a broader role in maintaining skin health and responding to environmental stressors.
