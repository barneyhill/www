## Function
LGALS7B encodes the protein galectin-7B, which is a member of the galectin family known for its involvement in cell-cell and cell-matrix interactions. Galectin-7B is primarily expressed in epithelial tissues, where it plays a significant role in various cellular processes, including apoptosis, cell proliferation, and differentiation. This protein is active in both the cytoplasm and the nucleus, where it influences critical biological processes such as wound healing and the regulation of immune responses.

In healthy human cells, galectin-7B contributes to maintaining cellular homeostasis by modulating apoptosis, a process of programmed cell death essential for removing damaged or unnecessary cells. This function is crucial for tissue remodeling and repair, particularly in epithelial tissues where rapid cell turnover is common. Galectin-7B's role in cell proliferation and differentiation further underscores its importance in tissue development and regeneration.

The protein's activity in the cytoplasm and nucleus allows it to participate in signaling pathways that regulate these cellular processes. By influencing cell-matrix interactions, galectin-7B also plays a part in maintaining the structural integrity of tissues, which is vital for proper organ function and overall organismal health.
