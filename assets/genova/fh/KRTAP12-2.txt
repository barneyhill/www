# KRTAP12-2

## Overview
KRTAP12-2 is a gene that encodes the keratin-associated protein 12-2, a member of the keratin-associated protein family, which is integral to the structural composition of hair. This protein is characterized by a high cysteine content, facilitating the formation of disulfide bonds that are crucial for the mechanical strength and resilience of hair fibers (Shibuya2004A). The keratin-associated protein 12-2 interacts with keratin intermediate filaments, playing a vital role in maintaining hair texture and quality (Shibuya2004A). The gene is specifically expressed in hair root cells, highlighting its specialized function in hair development and growth (Shibuya2004A).

## Function
KRTAP12-2 encodes keratin-associated protein 12-2, which plays a crucial role in the structural integrity of hair. This protein is part of the high-sulfur KAP gene family, characterized by a cysteine content of 16-30%, which is essential for forming disulfide bonds that contribute to the mechanical strength and resilience of hair fibers (Shibuya2004A). KRTAP12-2 is specifically expressed in hair root cells, indicating its active role in the hair cortex, where it interacts with keratin intermediate filaments. This interaction is vital for maintaining hair texture and quality (Shibuya2004A).

The KRTAP12 subfamily, including KRTAP12-2, contains motifs like CCVP, CCQP, and CCQPS, which are involved in the protein's structural functions. These motifs, although present at a lower density compared to other KAP subfamilies, are integral to the protein's ability to support hair structure (Shibuya2004A). The expression of KRTAP12-2 is restricted to hair root cells, suggesting a specialized function in hair formation and growth, with no significant expression detected in other tissues (Shibuya2004A). This specific expression pattern underscores its role in the development and maintenance of healthy hair.
