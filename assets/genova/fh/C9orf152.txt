# C9orf152

## Overview
C9orf152, or chromosome 9 open reading frame 152, is a gene located on the short arm of chromosome 9. It encodes a protein that is currently not well-characterized in terms of its specific biological function or classification into known protein categories such as kinases or receptors. Despite this, emerging research has highlighted its potential involvement in various cellular pathways and disease processes. The protein encoded by C9orf152 has been implicated in cancer biology, particularly in prostate and endometrial cancers, where it may serve as a biomarker for disease prognosis and recurrence (Liu2020Key; Wu2020A; Wang2023Characterizing). Additionally, C9orf152 is situated in a genomic region associated with 9q deletion syndrome, suggesting a possible role in neurodevelopmental disorders, although its precise contributions remain to be elucidated (Szalai2022Overlapping).

## Clinical Significance
C9orf152 has been implicated in several diseases and conditions through mutations, alterations in expression levels, and changes in its interactions. In prostate cancer, a multi-ancestry genome-wide association study identified a novel frameshift deletion in the C9orf152 gene specific to European populations, suggesting its role in prostate cancer susceptibility (Wang2023Characterizing). Additionally, C9orf152 is part of a 9-mRNA signature associated with increased risk of biochemical recurrence in prostate cancer patients, indicating its potential as a diagnostic marker for identifying high-risk individuals (Wu2020A).

In endometrial carcinoma, C9orf152 is positively correlated with the PIK3R3 gene, which is involved in the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway. This correlation suggests that C9orf152 may serve as a prognostic marker for patient survival in endometrial carcinoma (Liu2020Key).

C9orf152 is also located within a chromosomal region associated with 9q deletion syndrome, a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by intellectual disability and epilepsy. However, its specific clinical significance in this context is not well-defined (Szalai2022Overlapping).
