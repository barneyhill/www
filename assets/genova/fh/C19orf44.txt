# C19orf44

## Overview
C19orf44 is a gene located on chromosome 19 that encodes the protein known as chromosome 19 open reading frame 44. This protein is currently categorized as an uncharacterized protein, meaning its specific functions and mechanisms of action are not fully understood. However, it is implicated in various cellular processes, particularly in the context of cancer biology and metabolic reprogramming. The protein interacts with the testis-specific enzyme GAPDHS, which is involved in energy production pathways, suggesting a potential role in spermatogenesis and male fertility, as well as in cancer progression (Ershov2023Uncharacterized). C19orf44 is also part of a network of proteins associated with centriolar satellites, indicating its involvement in complex protein-protein interactions (Gheiratmand2019Spatial). While the clinical significance of C19orf44 is still being explored, its potential contributions to cancer prognosis and treatment are of particular interest (Ershov2023Uncharacterized).

## Clinical Significance
C19orf44, a gene located on chromosome 19, has been implicated in various cellular processes and potential disease associations, particularly in the context of cancer. Research suggests that C19orf44 may play a role in cancer-related metabolic reprogramming, possibly through its interaction with the testis-specific enzyme GAPDHS, which is involved in energy production pathways (Ershov2023Uncharacterized). This interaction could influence spermatogenesis and male fertility, as well as contribute to cancer progression.

The gene is part of a broader group of uncharacterized open reading frame (ORF) proteins that have been studied for their involvement in cancer-dependent protein-protein interactions (PPIs). These interactions are crucial for understanding the molecular pathways that drive oncogenic processes (Ershov2023Uncharacterized). Although the clinical significance of C19orf44 is not fully established, its potential role in cancer prognosis and treatment is being explored, with gene co-expression analysis highlighting its involvement in cancer biology (Ershov2023Uncharacterized).

Additionally, C19orf44 is located within a region on chromosome 19p13.11 that has been associated with genetic syndromes, although its specific contribution to these conditions remains unclear (Aten2009Split).

## Interactions
The C19orf44 protein is involved in various protein-protein interactions, particularly with the testis-specific multifunctional enzyme GAPDHS (glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase). This interaction is impaired, which may have implications in spermatogenesis, male fertility, and metabolic reprogramming in cancers (Ershov2023Uncharacterized). The study of C19orf44 is part of a broader analysis of open reading frame (ORF) proteins that may participate in cancer-dependent protein-protein interactions (PPIs) or PPIs perturbed by cancers. Gene co-expression analysis is used to explore these interactions, suggesting that C19orf44 is part of a complex network of interactions (Ershov2023Uncharacterized).

In the context of centriolar satellites, C19orf44 is mentioned in a study that maps protein interactions using BioID analysis.
