# KCNC4

## Overview
KCNC4 is a gene that encodes the protein potassium voltage-gated channel subfamily C member 4, which is a crucial component of the voltage-gated potassium channel family, specifically within the Shaw-related subfamily. This protein functions as a transmembrane channel, characterized by its six transmembrane segments and a pore-forming region, which are essential for its role in neuronal signaling. The KCNC4 protein is involved in the rapid repolarization phase of the action potential in neurons, facilitating the flow of potassium ions across the cell membrane in response to changes in membrane potential. This process is vital for maintaining the electrical excitability of neurons. The protein's activity can be modulated by post-translational modifications, such as phosphorylation, and it may exist in different isoforms due to alternative splicing, which can influence its physiological functions. Mutations or dysregulation of KCNC4 have been linked to neurological disorders, including epilepsy and ataxia, highlighting its significance in maintaining normal neuronal function (Qiu2020aarFDC).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations or altered expression of the KCNC4 gene, which encodes a voltage-gated potassium channel, have been associated with several neurological disorders. Disruptions in the normal function of this channel can lead to conditions such as epilepsy and ataxia. These disorders arise due to impaired ion flow, which affects nerve signal transmission and neuronal excitability. The precise mechanisms by which KCNC4 mutations contribute to these conditions are still under investigation, but they are believed to involve changes in the electrical properties of neurons that disrupt normal brain function. Research is ongoing to better understand the clinical implications of KCNC4 mutations and to explore potential therapeutic strategies for managing the associated neurological symptoms. Understanding the role of KCNC4 in these disorders could provide insights into the development of targeted treatments aimed at restoring normal ion channel function and improving patient outcomes.
