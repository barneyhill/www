# CYB561A3

## Overview
CYB561A3 is a gene that encodes the protein cytochrome b561 family member A3, which is part of the cytochrome b561 family known for its role in transmembrane electron transport. This protein is primarily involved in electron transfer processes using ascorbate as an electron donor and is localized in lysosomal membranes. It plays a crucial role in pigmentation by regulating melanin production in skin melanocytes, and its overexpression has been shown to decrease melanin levels, indicating its involvement in melanosome function and melanin biosynthesis pathways (Feng2024Integrative). Additionally, CYB561A3 is implicated in mitochondrial respiration and oxidative stress management through its Fe3+-reductase activity, contributing to cellular redox balance and iron metabolism (Asard2013Cytochromesb561:). The gene's expression is linked to skin pigmentation and is a target of genetic variants associated with pigmentation traits (Feng2024Integrative). Furthermore, CYB561A3 has been associated with various diseases, including cancer, due to its role in cellular redox processes and neurotransmitter regulation (Wang2023Identification; Guo2021The).

## Function
CYB561A3, a member of the cytochrome b561 family, is involved in transmembrane electron transport processes, primarily using ascorbate as an electron donor. This protein is localized in lysosomal membranes and is highly expressed in skin melanocytes, where it plays a significant role in pigmentation by regulating melanin production. Overexpression of CYB561A3 in MNT-1 cells, a model for studying pigmentation, significantly decreases melanin levels, suggesting its involvement in melanosome function and melanin biosynthesis pathways (Feng2024Integrative).

CYB561A3 is also implicated in mitochondrial respiration and melanin production, affecting the expression of genes related to these processes. It is associated with the regulation of pigmentation through its interaction with melanosomal proteins like TYRP1 (Feng2024Integrative). The gene is a target of the GWAS-SNP rs7948623, linked to skin pigmentation, indicating its potential role in pigmentation regulation (Feng2024Integrative).

In addition to its role in pigmentation, CYB561A3 is involved in oxidative stress management through its Fe3+-reductase activity, contributing to cellular redox balance and iron metabolism (Asard2013Cytochromesb561:).

## Clinical Significance
CYB561A3 has been implicated in various diseases due to its role in cellular redox processes and neurotransmitter regulation. In the context of cancer, CYB561A3 is noted to be essential for the proliferation of Burkitt's lymphoma cells, indicating its potential involvement in the progression of this type of cancer (Guo2021The). The study by Wang et al. highlights that CYB561A3 is highly expressed in B cells, suggesting a possible role in immune cell function and its association with hepatocellular carcinoma (Wang2023Identification).
