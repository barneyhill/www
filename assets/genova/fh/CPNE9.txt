# CPNE9

## Overview
CPNE9 is a gene that encodes the protein copine family member 9, which is part of the copine family known for its calcium-dependent phospholipid-binding capabilities. The protein is characterized by the presence of two C2 domains, which are essential for binding calcium ions, thereby facilitating interactions with phospholipids. This suggests a potential role in membrane trafficking and signal transduction processes. Additionally, CPNE9 contains an A domain that is implicated in mediating protein-protein interactions, which may influence various cellular processes. The structural composition of CPNE9, including its domain architecture, indicates its involvement in critical cellular functions, particularly those related to membrane dynamics and signaling pathways (Qiu2020aarFDC).
