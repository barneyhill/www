# ACP7

## Overview
ACP7 is a gene that encodes the protein acid phosphatase 7, which is a putative tartrate-resistant enzyme belonging to the histidine acid phosphatase family. This family of enzymes is characterized by a conserved RHGXRXP motif, essential for their enzymatic activity. The protein encoded by ACP7 is thought to function primarily as a phosphotyrosyl protein phosphatase, playing a significant role in the dephosphorylation processes within human cells. It is particularly noted for its potential involvement in bone metabolism and osteoclast function, given its tartrate-resistant nature and activity at neutral pH. The enzyme's sensitivity to fluoride and its presence in human osteoblastic cells suggest its importance in bone health, potentially influencing conditions such as osteoporosis through the modulation of protein phosphorylation (Wergedal1992Human).
