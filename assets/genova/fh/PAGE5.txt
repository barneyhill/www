# PAGE5

## Overview
PAGE5 is a gene that encodes the protein PAGE family member 5, which is part of the cancer/testis antigen family. This protein is classified as an intrinsically disordered protein (IDP), meaning it lacks a stable three-dimensional structure under physiological conditions. PAGE5 is primarily expressed in testicular tissue and certain cancer cells, where it is thought to play a role in spermatogenesis and potentially in various regulatory processes and signaling pathways. The protein's structural flexibility, characterized by transiently populated secondary structures such as alpha-helices and beta-strands, allows it to engage in diverse interactions, although it does not bind to double-stranded DNA like its family member PAGE4. The precise molecular functions and interaction partners of PAGE5 remain under investigation, with ongoing research needed to fully elucidate its role in cellular and organismal contexts (Hellman2011Characterization).

## Structure
PAGE5 is an intrinsically disordered protein (IDP) that lacks a stable three-dimensional structure under physiological conditions. Its primary structure is characterized by a sequence of amino acids that contribute to its disordered nature, with certain regions showing transiently populated secondary structures. NMR spectroscopy has identified transient alpha-helical and beta-strand conformations, particularly in the regions encompassing residues 65-77 and 29-42, respectively (Hellman2011Characterization). These transient structures are more pronounced at lower pH levels, suggesting potential molecular recognition features (MoRFs) that may undergo disorder-to-order transitions upon ligand binding (Hellman2011Characterization).

PAGE5 is highly soluble and behaves as a tetramer in size exclusion chromatography, although diffusion NMR experiments suggest it exists as a monomer in solution (Hellman2011Characterization). The protein's secondary structure is influenced by pH, with increased alpha-helical propensity observed at acidic pH, particularly in the region spanning residues 65-77 (Hellman2011Characterization). Despite its disordered nature, PAGE5 does not bind to double-stranded DNA, unlike its family member PAGE4 (Hellman2011Characterization).
