# MS4A15

## Overview
MS4A15 is a gene that encodes the protein membrane spanning 4-domains A15, a member of the MS4A family characterized by four transmembrane domains. This tetraspanin protein is primarily localized to the endoplasmic reticulum, where it plays a significant role in calcium homeostasis and lipid remodeling. The MS4A15 protein is involved in the regulation of calcium ion release and lipid metabolism, contributing to cellular processes such as ferroptosis resistance by altering lipid composition to protect against oxidative damage. The gene's expression has been linked to various clinical outcomes, particularly in cancer, where it influences cell survival and therapy resistance. MS4A15's involvement in immune regulation and its potential as a prognostic marker in lung cancer further underscore its clinical significance (Mattiola2021The; Xin2021MS4A15; Zheng2023Role).

## Structure
The MS4A15 protein is a member of the MS4A family, characterized by having four transmembrane-spanning domains, which is a common feature among these proteins (Mattiola2021The; Liang2001Identification). These domains allow the protein to traverse the cell membrane multiple times, contributing to its structural integrity and function. The MS4A family proteins, including MS4A15, share high structural identity in the first three transmembrane domains, although they exhibit diversity in size and sequence outside these regions (Mattiola2021The).

MS4A15 has a longer cytoplasmic region compared to other MS4A proteins, which may influence its interactions and functional roles within the cell (Mattiola2021The). The protein is localized to the endoplasmic reticulum, where it plays a role in calcium homeostasis and lipid remodeling, although specific details about its tertiary or quaternary structure are not provided (Xin2021MS4A15).

The MS4A proteins, including MS4A15, lack polar residues in their transmembrane regions, distinguishing them from tetraspanins, and they may form lateral associations with other proteins (Mattiola2021The). The second extracellular loops of MS4A proteins show little sequence homology, suggesting functional divergence within the family (Mattiola2021The). ## Function
The MS4A15 gene encodes a tetraspanin protein localized to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), where it plays a crucial role in regulating calcium (Ca2+) homeostasis and lipid remodeling. MS4A15 depletes luminal Ca2+ stores, leading to the reprogramming of membrane phospholipids into ferroptosis-resistant species. This process involves inhibiting lipid elongation and desaturation, resulting in the formation of shorter, more saturated ether lipids that protect phospholipids from oxidative damage (Xin2021MS4A15). The gene's overexpression is associated with a decrease in polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs) and an increase in monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFAs) and MUFA-plasmalogens, which act as reactive oxygen species (ROS) sinks, protecting cells from lipid peroxidation (Xin2021MS4A15).

MS4A15 is also involved in lipid droplet dynamics and is linked to pathways such as smooth muscle contraction, PPAR signaling, and arachidonic acid metabolism, all triggered by Ca2+ release (Xin2021MS4A15). The protein's role in lipid remodeling and Ca2+ regulation may be relevant to cancer, as disruptions in these pathways could influence cell survival and therapy resistance (Xin2021MS4A15). However, the specific function of MS4A15 in healthy human cells is not directly addressed in the available literature.

## Clinical Significance
The MS4A15 gene has been implicated in several clinical contexts, particularly in relation to cancer. Alterations in the expression of MS4A15 are associated with resistance to ferroptosis, a form of regulated cell death. This resistance is achieved through calcium-restricted lipid remodeling, which is significant in cancer biology as it can influence cell survival and therapy resistance (Xin2021MS4A15). In lung adenocarcinoma, a subtype of non-small cell lung cancer, MS4A15 expression is generally downregulated compared to normal tissues. Lower expression levels of MS4A15, along with other MS4A family genes, are linked to poorer overall survival rates, suggesting its potential as a prognostic indicator (Zheng2023Role; Zheng2022Role). The gene's expression is also associated with immune cell infiltration in the tumor microenvironment, indicating a role in immune regulation (Zheng2023Role). High expression of MS4A15 is correlated with decreased metastasis and increased survival in lung cancer patients, highlighting its potential role in cancer prognosis (Xin2021MS4A15). These findings suggest that MS4A15 could serve as a target for therapeutic interventions in cancer treatment.

## Interactions
MS4A15 is involved in interactions with several proteins, particularly those associated with calcium regulation in the endoplasmic reticulum (ER). Immunoprecipitation studies of FLAG-tagged MS4A15 from HEK293T cell lysates have shown that MS4A15 co-elutes with proteins such as IP3-receptors, TMEM33, ERLINs, DNAJs, and ARFs, which are involved in calcium regulation (Xin2021MS4A15). These interactions suggest that MS4A15 plays a role in modulating calcium homeostasis within the ER, contributing to its function in ferroptosis resistance.

MS4A15 may also function as an ion channel through its association with other subunits, similar to other members of the MS4A family, such as MS4A1, MS4A2, and MS4A12, which have known calcium-regulating abilities (Xin2021MS4A15). This potential ion channel activity is supported by the observed reduction in calcium release from ER stores in cells overexpressing MS4A15, indicating a mechanism by which MS4A15 limits lipid oxidation and contributes to ferroptosis resistance (Xin2021MS4A15). These interactions highlight the role of MS4A15 in calcium-restricted lipid remodeling, which is crucial for its protective function against ferroptosis.
