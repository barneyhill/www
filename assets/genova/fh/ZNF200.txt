# ZNF200

## Overview
ZNF200 is a gene that encodes the zinc finger protein 200, a member of the zinc finger protein family characterized by the presence of zinc-finger motifs. These motifs are small protein domains that facilitate interactions with DNA, RNA, or other proteins, playing a crucial role in gene regulation and expression (Centola1998Construction). Unlike some other zinc finger proteins, ZNF200 lacks additional domains such as LeR/SCAN or KRAB, which are often involved in transcriptional repression (Centola1998Construction). The protein is predominantly expressed in the testis, suggesting a role in reproductive biology (Centola1998Construction). ZNF200 has been implicated in cancer pathways, with studies indicating its involvement in cell cycle control and chromosomal replication, as well as potential interactions with proteins like G9a that contribute to tumorigenesis (Donkel2021Common; Wang2019The). Despite these associations, the precise biological functions and clinical implications of ZNF200 remain to be fully understood, warranting further investigation.

## Structure
ZNF200 is a zinc finger protein characterized by the presence of five zinc-finger motifs, which are small protein domains that typically facilitate binding to DNA, RNA, or other proteins (Centola1998Construction). These motifs are homologous to those found in other zinc-finger proteins such as ZNF210, ZNF213, FPM315, and ZNF174, although the homology is not as strong (Centola1998Construction). Unlike other zinc-finger proteins in its genomic region, ZNF200 does not contain additional recognizable domains such as the LeR/SCAN or KRAB domains, which are present in other proteins like ZNF210 and ZNF213 (Centola1998Construction). Sequence alignment suggests the presence of vestigial LeR/SCAN and KRAB domains, but significant sequence divergence has occurred, differentiating ZNF200 from these other proteins (Centola1998Construction).

The protein is primarily expressed in a tissue-specific manner, with predominant expression in the testis (Centola1998Construction). ## Clinical Significance
ZNF200 has been implicated in various diseases, primarily through its role in cancer and its interactions with other genes. Genetic association studies have identified ZNF200 as part of a network of genes involved in inflammatory responses and cancer pathways. These studies suggest that ZNF200 is associated with cell cycle control and chromosomal replication pathways, which are crucial in cancer development (Donkel2021Common). Although specific single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) in ZNF200 have not been linked to particular diseases in the GWAS Catalog, the gene's involvement in cancer pathways is notable (Donkel2021Common).

Research has also highlighted the interaction between ZNF200 and the protein G9a, which contributes to cancer cell generation and metastasis, indicating a potential role in tumorigenesis and cancer progression (Wang2019The). In thyroid carcinoma, ZNF200 shows higher expression in normal tissues compared to tumor tissues, suggesting its potential involvement in cancer suppression (Machnik2019The). Despite these associations, the exact molecular functions and clinical significance of ZNF200 in cancer remain to be fully elucidated, necessitating further research to understand its role in disease mechanisms (Machnik2019The).
