# PGA5

## Overview
PGA5 is a gene that encodes the precursor protein pepsinogen A5, which belongs to the aspartic protease family. This protein is primarily involved in the digestive process, where it is secreted by gastric chief cells in the stomach lining and subsequently converted into the active enzyme pepsin in the acidic environment of the stomach. Pepsin plays a crucial role in breaking down dietary proteins into peptides, facilitating nutrient absorption. The expression of PGA5 is particularly significant during the later stages of gastric development and is predominantly found in the fundic gland region of the stomach, where it is associated with genes involved in acid secretion. Beyond its digestive functions, PGA5 has been implicated in various cancers, with its expression levels and genetic alterations influencing disease prognosis and survival outcomes (Shen2020The; Benedetti2023Generation; Giobbe2020SARSCoV2; Kiernan2023Exploring).

## Function
PGA5 encodes pepsinogen A5, a precursor to the enzyme pepsin, which plays a critical role in the digestive process. In healthy human cells, pepsinogen A5 is secreted by gastric chief cells located in the stomach lining. Upon exposure to the acidic environment of the stomach, pepsinogen A5 is converted into its active form, pepsin. This conversion is essential for the breakdown of proteins into peptides, facilitating nutrient absorption and overall digestive health (Giobbe2020SARSCoV2).

The expression of PGA5 is associated with later stages of gastric development, as indicated by its presence in late fetal gastric assembloids (fGAs) and absence in early developmental stages. This suggests that PGA5 may be involved in the maturation of gastric tissues (Benedetti2023Generation). In the context of gastric function, PGA5 expression is highest in the fundic gland region of the stomach, where it is involved in the production of digestive enzymes. This region is also enriched with genes related to acid secretion, indicating a coordinated role in digestive processes (Kiernan2023Exploring). The expression of PGA5 is positively correlated with genes involved in acid secretion, highlighting its role in the digestive enzyme network (Kiernan2023Exploring).

## Clinical Significance
PGA5 (pepsinogen A5) has been implicated in various cancers, with its expression and genetic alterations playing significant roles in disease prognosis. In kidney renal clear cell carcinoma and kidney renal papillary cell carcinoma, PGA5 is associated with a favorable prognosis, correlating with prolonged survival times (Shen2020The). Conversely, in lung squamous cell carcinoma, prostate adenocarcinoma, and uterine corpus endometrial carcinoma, PGA5 is linked to a poor prognosis and shorter survival (Shen2020The).

The mutation rate of PGA5 across all cancer lines is relatively low, less than 2%, but it shows notable copy number variations (CNV) in different cancers. For instance, PGA5 exhibits more amplification in lung adenocarcinoma, esophageal carcinoma, and kidney chromophobe, while reductions are observed in bladder urothelial carcinoma, lung squamous cell carcinoma, rectum adenocarcinoma, and cholangiocarcinoma (Shen2020The). Despite these variations, CNV does not appear to affect PGA5 expression levels (Shen2020The).

PGA5 is also involved in several cancer-related pathways, such as the K-RAS signaling pathway, bile acid metabolism, and the mTOR pathway, which may contribute to its role in tumorigenesis (Shen2020The). These findings suggest that PGA5 could serve as a potential biomarker for cancer prognosis and treatment targets.
