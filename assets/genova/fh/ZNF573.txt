# ZNF573

## Overview
ZNF573 is a gene that encodes the zinc finger protein 573, which is a member of the zinc finger protein family. These proteins are characterized by their zinc finger domains, which facilitate DNA binding and are crucial for transcriptional regulation. Zinc finger protein 573 is involved in various cellular processes, including gene expression modulation, through its interaction with DNA and other molecular partners. The protein's structure is stabilized by zinc ions, which coordinate with cysteine and histidine residues to maintain the integrity of its zinc finger motifs. ZNF573 has been implicated in several clinical conditions, such as nuclear age-related cataract and certain cancers, highlighting its significance in both normal cellular function and disease pathogenesis (Wang2021Four; Yu2019Identification; Cai2022A).

## Clinical Significance
ZNF573 has been implicated in several diseases due to mutations and alterations in its expression. Notably, polymorphisms in ZNF573 have been associated with nuclear age-related cataract (NARC) in Chinese individuals. The rs3095726 polymorphism (Met523Val) in ZNF573 leads to structural changes in the protein, potentially affecting its function and contributing to abnormal growth and development of the human lens, which may result in cataracts (Wang2021Four). 

In the context of cancer, ZNF573 has been identified as a hub gene with altered expression in microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer. Its expression levels are significantly reduced in MSI-H compared to microsatellite stable (MSS) groups, suggesting a potential role in the pathogenesis of MSI-related colorectal cancer (Yu2019Identification). 

ZNF573 is also involved in lung cancer diagnostics. It is one of the key markers in a panel of autoantibodies used to develop a diagnostic model for non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC). The presence of ZNF573 autoantibodies in sera from patients with early-stage NSCLC shows significant differences compared to control samples, indicating its potential role in lung cancer diagnostics (Cai2022A).
