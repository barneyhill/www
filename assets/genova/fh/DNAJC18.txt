# DNAJC18

## Overview
DNAJC18 is a gene that encodes the protein DnaJ heat shock protein family (Hsp40) member C18, which is part of the Hsp40 class of molecular chaperones. These proteins are essential for maintaining protein homeostasis by assisting in the proper folding of nascent proteins and preventing the aggregation of misfolded proteins. DNAJC18 is primarily active in the cytoplasm, where it plays a critical role in cellular stress response mechanisms, ensuring proteins achieve and maintain their correct conformation. This function is vital for cellular resilience, particularly under stress conditions, and contributes to the overall maintenance of cellular homeostasis. The gene has been implicated in various cardiac and cancer-related conditions, highlighting its significance in both developmental and pathological contexts (Zoppino2018Comprehensive; Spielmann2022Extensive).

## Function
DNAJC18, a member of the DnaJ heat shock protein family, specifically belongs to the Hsp40 class of molecular chaperones. These proteins play a crucial role in maintaining protein homeostasis within cells. DNAJC18 assists in the proper folding of nascent proteins and prevents the aggregation of misfolded proteins, which is essential for cellular function and resilience under stress conditions. It is actively involved in the refolding of misfolded proteins, thereby contributing to the cellular stress response mechanisms.

In healthy human cells, DNAJC18 operates primarily in the cytoplasm, where it supports the maintenance of protein quality control. By ensuring proteins achieve and maintain their correct conformation, DNAJC18 helps sustain cellular processes and prevents the accumulation of potentially toxic protein aggregates. This activity is vital for cellular resilience, particularly under conditions of environmental or physiological stress, where the demand for protein folding and repair is heightened.

The molecular chaperone activity of DNAJC18 is integral to the broader cellular stress response, aiding in the protection and recovery of cells from stress-induced damage. This function underscores its importance in maintaining cellular homeostasis and overall organismal health.

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and alterations in the expression of the DNAJC18 gene have been implicated in various cardiac and cancer-related conditions. In the context of congenital heart disorders, DNAJC18 has been associated with ventricular septal defects (VSD) in mouse models. Homozygous deletion of DNAJC18 in mice results in severe structural cardiac malformations and early embryonic death, suggesting its critical role in cardiac development (Spielmann2022Extensive). The gene's loss of function is linked to increased left ventricular end-systolic volume and decreased systolic volume index, indicating its potential involvement in cardiac function and disease (Spielmann2022Extensive).

In cancer research, DNAJC18 has been noted for its downregulation across all breast cancer subtypes studied, although its specific role in cancer progression remains unclear (Zoppino2018Comprehensive). In oesophageal carcinoma, DNAJC18 expression is negatively correlated with methylation levels, with high expression associated with poorer overall patient survival, suggesting its potential as a prognostic marker (Pohl2019Gene). These findings highlight the gene's significance in both cardiac and cancer pathophysiology, warranting further investigation into its clinical implications.
