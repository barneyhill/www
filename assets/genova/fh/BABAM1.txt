# BABAM1

## Overview
BABAM1, also known as MERIT40, is a gene that encodes the protein BRISC and BRCA1 A complex member 1, which is integral to cellular processes involving DNA damage response and repair. The protein is a component of two critical complexes: the BRISC (BRCC36 isopeptidase complex) and the BRCA1-A complex. Within these complexes, it plays a pivotal role in maintaining genomic stability by participating in the repair of DNA double-strand breaks through homologous recombination. The BRISC complex is involved in deubiquitination processes, while the BRCA1-A complex is essential for sequestering BRCA1 away from DNA damage sites, thereby regulating its availability for repair functions. The involvement of BABAM1 in these complexes underscores its importance in cellular homeostasis and its potential implications in cancer susceptibility, particularly in breast and ovarian cancers (Lawrenson2016Functional; Rabl2020BRCA1A).

## Clinical Significance
Mutations and alterations in the expression of the BABAM1 gene have been implicated in the development of breast cancer. The gene is a member of the BRCA1-A complex, which is crucial for DNA repair through homologous recombination. In breast cancer, bi-allelic inactivation of homologous recombination (HR) genes, including BABAM1, is often necessary for the manifestation of homologous recombination deficiency (HRD). A study identified a case where a homozygous deletion of BABAM1, alongside a CHEK2 deletion, was observed in a RAD51-deficient breast cancer case. This deletion is significant as it impairs the recruitment of BRCA1 and RAD51, essential components of the DNA repair process, thereby contributing to cancer progression (Mutter2017Bi‐allelic).

While BABAM1 is involved in recruiting BRCA1 to sites of DNA damage, specific evidence linking its mutations or expression alterations to clinical conditions in ovarian cancer is not detailed. The focus in related studies is primarily on other genes like ABHD8, although BABAM1's role in DNA repair suggests its potential involvement in cancer susceptibility (Lawrenson2016Functional). Overall, disruptions in BABAM1's normal interactions and functions can significantly impact genomic stability and are associated with cancer development.

## Interactions
BABAM1, also known as MERIT40, is a component of the BRISC (BRCC36 isopeptidase complex) and the BRCA1-A complex. Within the BRISC complex, BABAM1 interacts with other subunits such as ABRAXAS2, BABAM2, BRCC3, and SHMT2. These interactions were identified using LuTHy and mN2H assays, which detected 82.4% of intra-complex interactions, suggesting a robust interaction network within the complex (Trepte2021A). 

In the context of the BRCA1-A complex, BABAM1 plays a role in DNA damage response and repair. It interacts with proteins such as BRCC36, BRCA1, and RAP80. The BRCA1-A complex forms a dimer of hetero-pentamers, where ABRAXAS, BRCC36, BRE, MERIT40 (BABAM1), and RAP80 are key components. This complex sequesters BRCA1 away from DNA damage sites, with the BRCA1 BRCT domain interacting with phosphorylated proteins, including ABRAXAS (Rabl2020BRCA1A). 

BABAM1 is also associated with genetic variants linked to cancer risk. SNP rs4808075, located in the BABAM1 gene, shows a strong association with serous ovarian cancer, highlighting its potential role in cancer susceptibility (Lawrenson2016Functional).
